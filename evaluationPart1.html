Evaluating ROMULUS

One of the ways in which we evaluate the functionality of ROMULUS is with a web-based survey. Participants are encouraged to be honest, and answer as descriptively as possible.

    Part 1: Evaluating the functionality of ROMULUS's features

  1. Modularisation aids in managing large ontologies. In ROMULUS, we offer a number of different module types. These can be viewed at Browse Ontologies. Which modules would you use?
    Separate endurants from perdurants in the ontologies: This is used when one wants to keep the entities that exist in full at all times (endurants) separate from entities with temporal parts that unfold over time (perdurants).

    Isolated branches of taxonomies of the ontologies for available subject domains support: Having modules that can be used for specific subject domains e.g., biomedical, business.

    More/less detailed versions of the ontologies: Having different variations of ontologies with fewer/more entities, object properties and axioms e.g., gfo-basic.
